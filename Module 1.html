What is Microsoft Copilot?
What is Microsoft Copilot?

Microsoft Copilot is an AI-powered assistant that helps you work smarter across the Microsoft 365 suite. It combines the power of large language models with your organization's data to help you create, communicate, and collaborate more effectively.

Conversation
Chat naturally to get answers and assistance
Creation
Draft documents, emails, and presentations
Web Search
Find information grounded in web sources
Data Analysis
Analyze spreadsheets and extract insights
Document Synthesis
Summarize and extract from documents
Translation
Translate content across languages
How to Access Microsoft Copilot

Access Microsoft 365 Copilot through the Microsoft 365 portal:

microsoft365.com

Copilot can also be accessed directly from Microsoft Teams and Outlook, if enabled by your IT department.

Enterprise Data Protection

While Microsoft Copilot and consumer AI tools like ChatGPT are both powered by advanced language models, Copilot offers critical Enterprise Data Protection that consumer tools cannot provide:

Your Data Stays Private

Your prompts, documents, and outputs remain within your organization's security boundary. They are not shared with other organizations or accessible to anyone outside your tenant.

Not Used for AI Training

Your data is never used by Microsoft to train, improve, or fine-tune AI models. What you share with Copilot stays with you.

Bottom line: When you use Microsoft Copilot with your work account, you get the power of advanced AI with the security and privacy guarantees your organization requires.

Copilot Chat vs Microsoft 365 Copilot

Microsoft offers different Copilot experiences. Here are the two main ones you should know:

Microsoft 365 Copilot Chat

What we're learning today

  • Included with Microsoft 365 subscription
  • Web-grounded (can search the internet)
  • No direct access to your organizational data
  • You paste or upload content to analyze
Microsoft 365 Copilot

Premium experience (additional license required)

  • Requires additional Copilot license
  • Direct integration with your Microsoft 365 data
  • Works inside Outlook, Teams, Word, Excel, PowerPoint
  • Can access your emails, calendar, files, and chats

Good news: The skills you learn today with Copilot Chat transfer directly to Microsoft 365 Copilot. Master the habits now, and you'll be ready when the full experience becomes available.

Responsible Use of AI

Before diving into Copilot's capabilities, it's essential to understand how to use AI responsibly and effectively.

Remember: You are always in control. Copilot gives you a starting point, but you provide the judgment, review the output, and make the final decisions.

Verify All Outputs

AI can make mistakes or "hallucinate" information. Always fact-check data, names, dates, and claims before using or sharing.

Protect Sensitive Data

While Copilot Chat is secure, be thoughtful about what you paste or upload. Follow your organization's data handling policies.

Human Judgment is Final

You are accountable for the work you produce. Use AI as a collaborator, but apply your expertise and judgment to the final output.

What Can Copilot Do for Me?

These seven habits turn common work friction into repeatable workflows. Each habit includes a "hero prompt" you can use immediately, plus follow-ups and tips to help you adapt Copilot to your daily work.

Remember: Copilot Chat is web-grounded and does not have access to your organizational data. You need to paste or upload content for analysis.

The 7 AI Habits

Select a habit below to explore how it works.

1 Use Copilot as Your Ask-Me-Anything Machine

The Challenge: You need quick answers on unfamiliar topics but don't want to spend hours reading.

Hero Prompt

Explain [topic] to me as if I'm a [beginner / intermediate / expert]. Include a simple analogy, 3 key points, and one common misconception to avoid.
Follow-ups
  • Give me a real-world example of how [topic] is applied in [industry/context].
  • What questions should I ask to deepen my understanding of this topic?
  • Give me a 3-bullet summary I can share with [audience].
Tips
  • Specify your expertise level for appropriately pitched explanations.
  • Ask for analogies to connect new concepts to familiar ones.
  • Request sources when accuracy matters.

2 Use Copilot as Your Research Assistant

The Challenge: Guessing keywords, opening tabs, and piecing together sources is slow and error-prone.

Hero Prompt

What's the latest on [topic/company/industry]? List the key updates, why each matter, and include 3-5 reputable sources with dates. Suggest follow-up questions.
Follow-ups
  • Explain the implications of the two most important updates for our business.
  • How could these updates affect our strategy or key competitors?
  • What related trends or cross-industry comparisons should I watch?
Tips
  • Define scope up front: audience, region, timeframe, and angle.
  • Ask for source title, publisher, and date to check credibility.
  • Open citations and verify before sharing.

3 Use Copilot as Your Email Assistant

The Challenge: Long threads, careful tone, and editing consume time; decisions get lost.

Hero Prompt

I need to write an email to [recipient/role] about [topic]. The tone should be [professional/friendly/formal]. Key points to include: [list]. Draft the email in ~150 words with a clear call-to-action.
Follow-ups
  • Make this shorter and more direct. Keep it under 100 words.
  • Suggest 3 subject line options that would get this email opened.
Tips
  • Copilot Chat cannot see your actual emails. Paste the email thread or draft you want to work with.
  • Specify tone and audience for better results.
  • Always review and personalize before sending.

4 Use Copilot as Your Meeting Assistant

The Challenge: Manual note-taking breaks focus; key details slip through the cracks.

Hero Prompt

I'm pasting a meeting transcript below. Please create meeting minutes with: 3-sentence summary, key decisions made, action items with owners and due dates, and open questions. Format for easy copy-paste into email. [Paste transcript here]
Follow-ups
  • What were the unresolved points? Suggest how to address each in a follow-up.
  • Turn the action items into a numbered checklist I can paste into a task manager.
Tips
  • Download or copy your meeting transcript and paste it into Copilot Chat.
  • Review and edit AI-generated minutes before sharing.

5 Content Creation

The Challenge: The blank page slows delivery; finding the right words and structure takes time.

Hero Prompt

I need to write a [document type: report/proposal/article] about [topic] for [audience]. Create a detailed outline with 5-7 sections, each with 2-3 key points. Suggest a compelling opening hook.
Follow-ups
  • Write the first draft for section [X] in approximately [word count] words.
  • Make this section more engaging by adding a relevant example or analogy.
Tips
  • Say who the audience is, the goal, and the target length.
  • Build section by section rather than asking for everything at once.
  • Copilot Chat can also create Microsoft 365 documents (Word, Excel, PowerPoint) directly.

6 Use Copilot as Your Document Insights

The Challenge: Long, dense documents take time to digest; key details are easy to miss.

Hero Prompt

I'm uploading/pasting a document. Please summarize it in 150-200 words for [audience]. Include: main purpose, 5 key takeaways, any deadlines or decisions mentioned, and questions I should ask after reading. [Paste or upload document]
Follow-ups
  • Create a 10-item FAQ based on this document. Keep answers concise.
  • What are the most important numbers or data points in this document?
Tips
  • Upload PDF or Word documents directly, or paste the text content for analysis.
  • Specify your audience for appropriately tailored summaries.
  • Ask follow-up questions to dive deeper into specific sections.

7 Use Copilot as Your Data Analyst

The Challenge: Working with data is slow without analyst skills; errors creep in.

Hero Prompt

I'm pasting data below. Please analyze it and tell me: 3 key insights, any patterns or trends, anomalies or outliers worth investigating, and 2 recommended actions based on the data. [Paste data or table]
Follow-ups
  • What chart type would best visualize the main trend? Describe what it would look like.
  • What additional data would help me understand this better?
Tips
  • Keep datasets small and clean for best results.
  • Paste data as a table or upload a CSV/Excel file.
  • Double-check numbers against your source before acting.
Tips & Tricks
1 Crafting Effective Prompts

The difference between a vague prompt and a well-crafted prompt:

Bad Prompt

Tell me about AI adoption

Result: Generic, unfocused response.

Good Prompt

Goal: List the top 3 challenges teams face when adopting AI tools. Context: I'm an AI Champion helping my department adopt Copilot. Expectations: For each challenge, explain why it happens and give one practical strategy. Keep each to 2-3 sentences.

Result: Structured, actionable response.

Anatomy of a Good Prompt

Use the Goal → Context → Expectations framework:

ElementWhat to IncludeExample
GoalWhat you want the AI to produce"List the top 3 challenges..."
ContextBackground, audience, key facts"I'm an AI Champion helping..."
ExpectationsTone, format, length, must include/avoid"Keep each to 2-3 sentences..."

Pro tip: You don't need all three every time, but the more specific you are, the better your results.

2 Personalise Your Copilot

Set up custom instructions to make Copilot's responses more relevant to you by default.

How to Set Up Custom Instructions

  1. Open Copilot Chat and click on the menu (the "..." on the top right)
  2. Look for "Personalization" or "Custom Instructions"
  3. Add information about your role, preferences, and common tasks
  4. Save your settings

Example Custom Instructions

I am a [Your Role] at [Company Name], a [brief company description]. I frequently work on [common tasks]. When responding: - Use a professional, clear tone - Keep responses focused and actionable - Format for easy scanning when appropriate - Ask clarifying questions when requests are vague - Cite sources when relevant

Why personalise? Custom instructions save you from repeating the same context in every prompt.

3 Save to Prompt Gallery

When you find a prompt that works well, save it for quick reuse.

Prompt Worth Saving

What are the latest Microsoft Copilot features or updates released in the past month? List 3-5 key updates, explain what each does in plain language, and suggest which roles would benefit most. Include sources with dates.

How to Save a Prompt

  1. Run a prompt that gives you a great result
  2. Hover over your prompt to reveal the bookmark icon
  3. Click Save prompt
  4. Give it a descriptive name (e.g., "Copilot Feature Updates")
  5. Your prompt is saved to Your prompts tab

How to Access Saved Prompts

  1. Open the Copilot Prompt Gallery
  2. Navigate to the Your prompts tab
  3. Click on any saved prompt to view, edit, or run it

Pro tip: The gallery also has Microsoft-curated prompts you can use as-is or customise.
