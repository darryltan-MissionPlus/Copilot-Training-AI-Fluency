Demystifying Copilot
What is Microsoft Copilot?

Microsoft Copilot is an AI-powered assistant that helps you work smarter across the Microsoft 365 suite. It combines the power of large language models with your organization's data to help you create, communicate, and collaborate more effectively.

Key Differentiator: Your data is private and not accessible by the service provider. Microsoft 365 Copilot uses your organization's data responsibly, with enterprise-grade security and compliance.

Enterprise Data Protection in Microsoft 365 Copilot

Microsoft 365 Copilot vs Other Generative AI Tools

What makes Microsoft Copilot different from consumer AI tools like ChatGPT or Gemini?

Data Privacy
Your data is protected and not used to train AI models
M365 Integration
Works seamlessly within Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Outlook, and Teams
Organizational Data
Access and work with your organization's files and communications
Copilot Chat vs Microsoft 365 Copilot

Understanding the difference helps you choose the right tool for each task:

Copilot Chat FREE
  • Web-based AI assistant at microsoft365.com
  • Works with content you paste or upload
  • Great for general questions and web research
  • No direct access to your organization's data
  • Use the paste/upload pattern for personal files
Microsoft 365 Copilot LICENSED
  • Integrated into Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Outlook, Teams
  • Direct access to your emails, files, chats, meetings
  • Understands your organization's context
  • Can search across your work data automatically
  • Enterprise security and compliance built-in
Try This Prompt!

Here's your first prompt to experience M365 Copilot's power. Use this in Copilot Chat to get a personalized daily digest:

Daily Digest for [today's date] What should be on my radar based on emails, chats and meetings from [yesterday]? Include: 1) A summary of key updates 2) A table of action items sorted by due date or priority, with owners and context 3) Any decisions made and open questions
Why this works: With M365 Copilot, you don't need to paste or upload anything. Copilot automatically accesses your emails, calendar, and chats to generate this digest.
What Can Copilot Do for Me?

Transform your daily work with these 7 AI Habits. Each habit represents a key area where Copilot can save you time and boost your productivity.

1 Personal Assistant H1

Start each day with clarity. Use Copilot as your personal assistant to get a quick overview of what's ahead and what needs your attention.

Hero Prompt: Daily Digest

Summarize my emails, meetings, and files from the past 24 hours. What are the key items I need to address today? Prioritize by urgency and importance.
Follow-up Prompts
  • "What emails require my response today?"
  • "Are there any scheduling conflicts in my calendar this week?"
  • "Draft a response to [person]'s email about [topic]"
  • "What meetings can I prepare for in advance?"
Tips for Success
  • Run your Daily Digest first thing each morning
  • Save the prompt to your Copilot Prompt Gallery for quick access
  • Customize the time range based on your schedule
  • Use follow-ups to dive deeper into specific items
Other Use Cases
  • Weekly review: "Summarize my key accomplishments and pending items from this week"
  • Catch-up after time off: "What did I miss while I was out? Summarize key updates from [dates]"
  • Project status: "What's the latest on [project name] based on recent emails and meetings?"

2 Email Assistant H2

Tame your inbox with Copilot. Quickly summarize long email threads, draft professional responses, and stay on top of your communications.

Hero Prompt: Email Summary

Summarize this email thread into 5 bullets. Include: 1) Key discussion points 2) Action items with owners and due dates 3) Decisions made 4) Open questions 5) Next steps
Follow-up Prompts
  • "Draft a reply acknowledging the action items assigned to me"
  • "Make the tone more formal/casual"
  • "Shorten this to 3 sentences"
  • "Add a note about [additional context]"
Tips for Success
  • Use Copilot in Outlook for direct integration with your emails
  • Specify the tone you want (professional, friendly, urgent)
  • Always review and personalize before sending
  • Use "Draft with Copilot" for new emails from scratch
Other Use Cases
  • Draft decline emails: "Write a polite decline for this meeting request, suggesting an alternative time"
  • Follow-up reminders: "Draft a gentle follow-up for my email from last week about [topic]"
  • Sensitive communications: "Help me respond to this feedback constructively and professionally"

3 Meeting Assistant H3

Make every meeting count. Use Copilot to prepare beforehand, stay engaged during, and follow up effectively after meetings.

Hero Prompt: Meeting Recap

Using this meeting transcript/notes [paste or link], draft: 1) a 3-sentence summary 2) decisions made 3) action items with owners and due dates 4) open questions Keep the tone professional and mark anything ambiguous as [Unclear]. Format for easy copy-paste into email.
Follow-up Prompts
  • "What did I miss in the last 10 minutes? Summarize key points and decisions"
  • "Generate a meeting summary with action items and owners"
  • "Draft follow-up email based on what we discussed"
  • "What were the main disagreements or concerns raised?"
Tips for Success
  • Enable Copilot transcription in Teams for real-time summaries
  • Use Copilot during meetings to catch up if you join late
  • Review the AI-generated recap before sending to attendees
  • Ask Copilot to highlight unresolved issues for follow-up
Other Use Cases
  • Agenda creation: "Create an agenda for a 30-minute project status meeting"
  • Meeting notes: "Format these rough notes into professional meeting minutes"
  • Stakeholder prep: "What should I know about [person] before our meeting?"

4 Research Assistant H4

Find answers faster. Use Copilot to search across your organization's knowledge, summarize findings, and gather insights from multiple sources.

Hero Prompt: Research Query

Search our organization's files and emails for information about [topic]. Summarize the key findings, identify who has expertise in this area, and list relevant documents I should review.
Follow-up Prompts
  • "Compare these two approaches and list pros and cons"
  • "What are the latest updates on this topic from the past month?"
  • "Who else should I talk to about this?"
  • "Summarize this document in 3 key points"
Tips for Success
  • Be specific about the time frame and scope of your search
  • Ask Copilot to cite its sources so you can verify
  • Use follow-up questions to dive deeper into specific findings
  • Combine with web search for external context
Other Use Cases
  • Policy lookup: "What is our company policy on [topic]?"
  • Precedent search: "Have we done something similar to [project] before?"
  • Expert finding: "Who in our organization has worked on [topic]?"

5 Content Creator H5

Create with confidence. Use Copilot to draft documents, presentations, and other content faster while maintaining quality and consistency.

Hero Prompt: Content Draft

Create a [document type] about [topic] for [audience]. Include: - Purpose: [what it should achieve] - Tone: [professional/friendly/persuasive] - Length: [approximate length] - Key points to cover: [main topics]
Follow-up Prompts
  • "Make this more concise, aim for 50% shorter"
  • "Add more detail to section 2"
  • "Rewrite this for a technical audience"
  • "Suggest 3 alternative headlines/titles"
Tips for Success
  • Use Copilot in Word for seamless document creation
  • Provide examples of the style you want
  • Start with an outline, then expand each section
  • Use "Rewrite" to try different approaches
Other Use Cases
  • Presentations: "Create a 10-slide presentation on [topic] with speaker notes"
  • Reports: "Turn these bullet points into a professional report"
  • Social posts: "Write a LinkedIn post announcing [achievement]"

6 Document Insights H6

Understand any document instantly. Use Copilot to summarize long documents, extract key information, and answer questions about content.

Hero Prompt: Document Summary

Summarize this document. Include: 1) Main purpose and key message 2) Critical findings or recommendations 3) Action items or next steps 4) Any risks or concerns mentioned 5) Questions I should follow up on
Follow-up Prompts
  • "What are the financial implications mentioned?"
  • "List all the deadlines mentioned in this document"
  • "What are the main arguments for and against?"
  • "Create a one-page executive summary"
Tips for Success
  • With Copilot Chat, upload or paste the document first
  • Ask specific questions rather than generic "summarize"
  • Request information in the format you need (bullets, table, narrative)
  • Compare multiple documents by uploading them together
Other Use Cases
  • Contract review: "What are the key terms and obligations in this contract?"
  • Policy comparison: "Compare these two policy documents and highlight differences"
  • Report extraction: "Extract all statistics and data points from this report"

7 Data Analyst H7

Make sense of your data. Use Copilot to analyze spreadsheets, identify patterns, and create visualizations that tell the story.

Hero Prompt: Data Analysis

Analyze this data and provide: 1) Key trends and patterns 2) Notable outliers or anomalies 3) Top performers and areas of concern 4) Recommendations based on the findings 5) Suggested visualizations to present this data
Follow-up Prompts
  • "Create a chart showing [specific comparison]"
  • "What formula would calculate [specific metric]?"
  • "Highlight rows where [condition is met]"
  • "Predict next quarter based on this trend"
Tips for Success
  • Use Copilot in Excel for direct data manipulation
  • Describe what you want to see, not how to do it
  • Ask Copilot to explain formulas it creates
  • Verify calculations on a sample before applying widely
Other Use Cases
  • Budget analysis: "Compare actual vs budget and highlight variances over 10%"
  • Sales data: "Show monthly sales trends by region"
  • Survey results: "Summarize the key findings from this survey data"
Tips & Tricks
1 Crafting Effective Prompts

The difference between a vague prompt and a well-crafted prompt:

Bad Prompt

Tell me about AI adoption

Result: Generic, unfocused response.

Good Prompt

Goal: List the top 3 challenges teams face when adopting AI tools. Context: I'm an AI Champion helping my department adopt Copilot. Expectations: For each challenge, explain why it happens and give one practical strategy. Keep each to 2-3 sentences.

Result: Structured, actionable response.

Anatomy of a Good Prompt

Use the Goal → Context → Expectations framework:

ElementWhat to IncludeExample
GoalWhat you want the AI to produce"List the top 3 challenges..."
ContextBackground, audience, key facts"I'm an AI Champion helping..."
ExpectationsTone, format, length, must include/avoid"Keep each to 2-3 sentences..."

Pro tip: You don't need all three every time, but the more specific you are, the better your results.

2 Personalise Your Copilot

Set up custom instructions to make Copilot's responses more relevant to you by default.

How to Set Up Custom Instructions

  1. Open Copilot Chat and click on the menu (the "..." on the top right)
  2. Look for "Personalization" or "Custom Instructions"
  3. Add information about your role, preferences, and common tasks
  4. Save your settings

Example Custom Instructions

I am a [Your Role] at [Company Name], a [brief company description]. I frequently work on [common tasks]. When responding: - Use a professional, clear tone - Keep responses focused and actionable - Format for easy scanning when appropriate - Ask clarifying questions when requests are vague - Cite sources when relevant

Why personalise? Custom instructions save you from repeating the same context in every prompt.

3 Save to Prompt Gallery

When you find a prompt that works well, save it for quick reuse.

Prompt Worth Saving

What are the latest Microsoft Copilot features or updates released in the past month? List 3-5 key updates, explain what each does in plain language, and suggest which roles would benefit most. Include sources with dates.

How to Save a Prompt

  1. Run a prompt that gives you a great result
  2. Hover over your prompt to reveal the bookmark icon
  3. Click Save prompt
  4. Give it a descriptive name (e.g., "Copilot Feature Updates")
  5. Your prompt is saved to Your prompts tab

How to Access Saved Prompts

  1. Open the Copilot Prompt Gallery
  2. Navigate to the Your prompts tab
  3. Click on any saved prompt to view, edit, or run it

Pro tip: The gallery also has Microsoft-curated prompts you can use as-is or customise.
